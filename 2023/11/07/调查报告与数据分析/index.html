Description：Solving urban air quality problems is of great significance. People can choose cleaner transportation methods and reduce pollutant emissions by obtaining real-time and accurate air quality data. This helps to improve the health status of residents, reduce environmental pollution, and drive cities towards more sustainable

development.

可视化：

Most of the surveyed people are 20-30 years old. They come from all over the country, have stable jobs, use mobile phones for a long time every day, and travel frequently. They will inquire about air quality for various things, and are willing to use related applications.

Some people are so concerned about air quality, especially those with special health needs, that they may take additional protective measures, such as wearing high-efficiency filter masks. In addition, social pressures and cultural factors can also influence travel decisions, and some people feel that social expectations make it difficult for them to adhere to environmental principles. This highlights how in urban environments, people’s travel decisions are influenced by a variety of factors, from personal health to social pressures.

Users’ level of concern and concerns about air quality vary from person to person. Some users are very concerned about air quality, especially for the health of their families, while others are less concerned, possibly because they trust the city government’s improvement measures. In addition, users’ concerns about data privacy and security were also a common theme, indicating a high level of concern for privacy protection when using apps. In addition, the positive response of users to incentives is also noteworthy, suggesting that economic or incentive factors can encourage people to travel more environmentally friendly.
